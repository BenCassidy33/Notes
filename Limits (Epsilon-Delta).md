## Limits and Epsilon-Delta Proofs
#### Example and Proof:
$$
\textnormal{Given: } f(x) = \lim_{x \rarr 2} (3x+1) \\ \textnormal{Prove that: } f(x) = 7 
$$

$$
    \textnormal{Eg: } \lim_{x \rarr 2} (3x+1) = 7
$$

<br />
<br />

$$
\begin{aligned}
&\textnormal{Given:} \quad \epsilon > 0 \\~\\
&\textnormal{We Choose:} \quad \delta = \frac{\epsilon}{3} \\~\\
&\textnormal{Suppose:} \quad0<|x-2|<\delta \\~\\
&\textnormal{Check:} \quad |3x+1-7|
\end{aligned}
$$

<br />
<br />

$$
\textnormal{Proof:} \\~\\

\begin{aligned}
\newline
1. &\quad |3x+1-7| = |3x-6| \\~\\
2. &\quad = 3|x-2| \\~\\
3. &\quad 3|x-2| < 3\times\delta \\~\\
4. &\quad 3 \times \frac{\epsilon}{3} = \epsilon \because \cancel{3} \times \frac{\epsilon}{\cancel{3}}

\end{aligned}
$$
